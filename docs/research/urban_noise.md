# Monitoring of urban noise

This study describes the design and field testing of a low-cost Mobile Internet of Things (M-IoT) platform for real-time urban noise monitoring and mapping. The system integrates portable nodes equipped with MEMS noise sensors and GSM communication to collect georeferenced sound-pressure data and transmit them to an online GIS platform that produces dynamic heat maps. A five-day pilot in downtown San Salvador, El Salvador, demonstrated its practicality: morning noise levels generally stayed below 60 dB, while afternoon measurements rose to 70–90 dB, with occasional peaks near 110 dB around busy intersections and Libertad Park. These results reveal a strong link between traffic density, pedestrian activity, and elevated acoustic pollution. Because the hardware is inexpensive and easily deployed, the approach is well suited to resource-constrained municipalities. By providing continuous, location-specific noise data, the system enables local authorities and planners to identify hotspots, implement targeted mitigation policies, and support healthier, more livable urban environments.
